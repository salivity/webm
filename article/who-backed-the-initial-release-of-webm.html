Who Backed the Initial Release of WebM?

The initial release of the WebM audio-video media file format was primarily backed by Google, Mozilla, and Opera Software, alongside a broad coalition of over forty industry-leading technology companies and publishers. Launched in May 2010 during the Google I/O conference, the WebM project was introduced as an open-source, royalty-free alternative to proprietary video formats for HTML5 video. The initiative aimed to provide a high-quality video compression format that was freely accessible to developers, web browsers, and content creators without the burden of licensing fees.

The Core Backers and Their Motivations

The driving force behind WebM’s debut consisted of major tech entities invested in the growth of an open web.

  • Google: Google played the foundational role by acquiring On2 Technologies in early 2010, the company that developed the VP8 video codec. Google open-sourced the VP8 technology and funded the development of WebM, integrating it directly into the Chromium browser project and YouTube.
  • Mozilla: As a staunch advocate for open web standards, Mozilla co-sponsored the launch. Mozilla sought a high-quality video format for the Firefox browser that did not require paying hefty licensing royalties to the MPEG-LA consortium for the competing H.264 codec.
  • Opera Software: Opera joined as an initial launch partner, immediately pledging support for the format within its Opera web browser to ensure its users had access to native, plug-in-free web video.

Industry Coalition and Hardware Support

Beyond the core software and browser developers, the initial release of WebM was supported by a vast ecosystem of hardware and software manufacturers. More than 40 companies announced their commitment to the format at launch. Notable hardware and semiconductor partners included:

  • AMD, NVIDIA, and Intel: These graphics and processor giants committed to integrating hardware acceleration for WebM video playback into their future chipsets.
  • Qualcomm and ARM: Mobile processor designers backed the format to ensure efficient, battery-friendly WebM decoding on smartphones and mobile devices.
  • Logitech: The peripherals manufacturer supported the initiative to enhance web-based video communication and webcam compatibility.
